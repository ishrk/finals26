Featured Spots

Gilchrist

Located on the Atlantic City Boardwalk, Gilchrist's is one of the top breakfast destinations with a classic diner offering something for everyone. You can order from their huge selection of breakfast foods, but the one thing you have to get every time is their famous hotcakes. These are very thin and soft pancakes that soak up syrup perfectly. You can order the "hungry man's combo #2," which came with hotcakes, 2 eggs cooked however I wanted, my choice of meat (sausage), and home fries. This was the perfect breakfast.

White House Subs

White House Subs has been around since 1932 and has served as the primary sandwich destination in Atlantic City. Known for their huge portions, even their half orders are bigger than expected. So come hungry! The bread is made fresh every day at the Formica Freitag Bakery across the street, which helps to create the high quality/tradition of their sandwiches. The menus are extensive, including items such as the "White House Special" and an outstanding meatball sub. With the average sandwich costing about $10-$12 each, it makes a great value for the price and size.

Setaara

Setaara provides an eclectic, one-of-a-kind culinary experience by mixing Afghan and French food in an internationally themed environment. The Afghan portion of the menu is most impressive: examples are bolani (crispy stuffed flatbreads with a bright green chutney), uzbeki palau (a wonderful lamb shank on fragrant pilaf rice), and mantu (light dumplings steamed to perfection and covered with yogurt and lentils). Plus, there is no charge for bringing your own liquor to accompany your meal.

About Atlantic Bites

Atlantic City is one of the most misrepresented cities in America. Visitors come for the casinos; residents know something far richer, a community built on culture, resilience, and incredible food. Atlantic Bites is a guide to that other city: the neighborhoods, kitchens, and people who make AC more than just a boardwalk.
