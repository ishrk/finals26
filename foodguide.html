The Food Guide

Atlantic City's food scene is as layered as the city itself. Beyond the buffets and celebrity chef restaurants inside the casinos, there's a real dining culture built by local families and longtime residents. You'll find fresh Jersey Shore seafood pulled straight from the Atlantic, soul food rooted in the city's deep Black community history, and international kitchens that reflect the immigrant families who have called AC home for generations. This guide skips the tourist traps and points you toward the spots that actually matter.

Dock's Oyster House

Seafood

Open since 1897, Dock's Oyster House is the oldest restaurant in Atlantic City and one of the oldest in New Jersey. It has survived Prohibition, the rise and fall of the casino era, and every economic shift the city has been through. The menu is classic Jersey Shore seafood of raw oysters, clam chowder, broiled flounder done with the kind of confidence that only comes from over a century of practice. This is not a trendy spot. It is an institution, and the locals treat it like one.

Kelsey & Kim's Southern Comfort

Soul Food

Kelsey & Kim's is a family-owned soul food kitchen that has become one of the most beloved spots in Atlantic City. The fried chicken is crispy and well-seasoned, the mac and cheese is made from scratch, and the collard greens taste like they've been cooking all day because they have. It's the kind of food that reminds you why home cooking will always beat anything you can get at a hotel restaurant. Small space, big flavors, and a warmth that makes you feel like a regular the first time you walk in.

Chef Vola's

International

Chef Vola's is the kind of place you only find out about if someone local tells you. There's no sign on the door, reservations are hard to get, and it's BYOB, but none of that stops it from being one of the most talked-about Italian restaurants on the East Coast. Located in a basement on Albion Place, it has been run by the Esposito family since 1921. The pasta is housemade, the portions are enormous, and the atmosphere feels like you've been invited to someone's grandmother's dinner table in Naples.

Flying Fish Cafe

Seafood

Flying Fish Cafe brings a more modern approach to Jersey Shore seafood without losing what makes local fish spots worth visiting. The menu changes based on what's fresh and in season, which keeps it honest. Grilled swordfish, steamed clams, and a rotating raw bar make up the core of what they do well. It's a step up from the classic diner-style seafood shacks but still feels accessible, no dress code, no attitude, just good fish cooked by people who know what they're doing. A solid pick for a relaxed dinner away from the boardwalk noise.
